    Unit 6 Seminar - The Debate - Generative AI and its Impact on Network Security

    Slide Title: Enhancing Network Security with Generative AI

    Introduction:
    Generative AI offers significant potential to streamline network security operations, automating routine tasks and empowering human resources to focus on complex security challenges and strategic planning.

    Example: Anomaly Detection in Network Traffic

    1. Data Collection:
    Collects vast amounts of historical network traffic data, including packet headers, flow information, and communication patterns. Incorporates data from other sources such as server logs, user activity records, and threat intelligence feeds.

    2. Training the Model:
    Utilises machine learning techniques to train a generative model on the collected data to learn normal patterns of network behaviour. Identifies typical traffic patterns, usual data transfer rates, common protocols, and standard communication pathways within the network.

    3. Anomaly Detection:
    Continuously monitors incoming network traffic in real-time.
    Flags anomalies as potential security threats when deviations from the learned normal behaviour are detected.
    Anomalies could include unusual spikes in data transfer, unexpected access attempts, or communication patterns that differ significantly from the norm.

    4. Alert Generation:
    Generates alerts and prioritises them based on the perceived severity of the deviation.
    Prioritises alerts related to suspicious activities in critical network segments or attempts to access sensitive data.

    5. Automated Response or Human Intervention:
    Triggers automated responses or escalates the alert to human analysts for further investigation, based on the severity of the detected anomaly.
    Automated responses may include blocking suspicious IP addresses, quarantining infected devices, or initiating temporary access restrictions.

    6. Feedback Loop:
    Continues to learn and adapt to new patterns and threats.
    Human analysts provide feedback by validating the alerts and adjusting the model's parameters accordingly.
    This iterative process improves the accuracy and effectiveness of the anomaly detection system over time.

    Real-World Application:
    Application in Financial Institutions:
    Major financial institutions deploy anomaly detection systems to protect their networks against cyber threats.
    These systems analyse network traffic in real-time, leveraging generative AI algorithms to detect suspicious activities such as data exfiltration attempts, DDoS attacks, or insider threats.
    Example: A bank might use generative AI-powered anomaly detection to monitor its network for unusual patterns of data access, login attempts from unfamiliar locations, or abnormal data transfer rates.

    Conclusion:
    The implementation of generative AI-driven anomaly detection systems represents a significant advancement in network security operations, enabling organisations to enhance their overall security posture, reduce response times, and focus human resources on higher-value security tasks.
