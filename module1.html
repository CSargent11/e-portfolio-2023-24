Module 1.

Over this 12 week module we will be taught to explore and investigate the fundamental theories and practices of computing, then gradually explore the trends and current legislative, regulatory and ethical developments in the field of computer science, which are used to minimise computer technology related threats and personal data breaches.
These are the aims from this module: 1. Examine the professional roles, skillsets, ethical responsibilities of computer science professionals. 2. Examine the relevant computing architectures, practices, applications, data representation and standards in both hardware and software perspectives 3. Develop an understanding of key computational theories and programming concepts 5. Develop an increasing confidence in applyting efficient algorithm and programming practices 6. Develop an ethos of professional development, highlighting major ethical-social-corporate responsibilities underpinning the concepts of threats, securities and global impacts prevailing in recent technilogical innovations.
